# Blog Post: I Wrote a 50-Page Business Proposal as an Engineer — Here's Why

By Max Ellis | April 2026

## Key Takeaways
- Engineers have a unique vantage point on product opportunities — but only if they share it
- Ideas die in Slack threads unless someone champions them
- Different stakeholders need different pitches

## Content

Engineers don't usually write business proposals. Max did — a 50-page document outlining product strategy, marketing positioning, and a sales approach.

### The Context

The company was at a crossroads. Projects were moving slowly, a key deal had fallen through, and morale was low. The AI landscape was exploding and competitors were shipping AI features. There was an enormous gap between what the company could be doing with AI and what they were doing.

### Why an Engineer?

The engineers around Max had genuinely good ideas. In hallway conversations and 1:1s, people would describe features that could meaningfully change the product. But none of them were raising these ideas formally. The ideas lived and died in Slack threads and coffee chats. Max decided to collect them, flesh them out, and put them into a format that leadership could act on.

### The Proposal

Max spent a Friday afternoon, the entire weekend, and most of his evenings the following week writing the proposal — all on his own time. The core thesis was simple: rebrand as an AI-driven marketing automation company through a suite of small, composable AI features that build on each other. He outlined three product suites composed of 15 AI-driven projects, along with a strategy for positioning and selling them.

### Selling It Internally

Writing the proposal was the easy part. Selling it internally was a crash course in stakeholder management. He started with his manager, who escalated it. The software architect did the same. He quickly learned that different stakeholders need different pitches — engineering cared about feasibility, sales cared about positioning, product cared about user value. He created tailored slide decks for each audience.

### What Happened

The core thesis — shifting toward AI-driven products — was adopted as company direction. The sales strategy was implemented. The first project shipped was the subject line optimizer, which drove a 17% increase in email open rates. Max went from writing the proposal to building the product.

### What He Learned

Every engineer he talked to had ideas worth hearing. But they assumed someone else would raise them, or that it wasn't their place. Engineers have a unique vantage point — they see the technical possibilities before anyone else. That perspective is incredibly valuable to sales, marketing, and product — but only if they share it.
